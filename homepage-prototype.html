Key Insight

New York and London are separated by just 1.2 points, but they arrive at nearly the same number through almost completely non-overlapping strengths. New York leads in vertical scale (347 skyscrapers to London's 44) and corporate mass ($8.7T market cap). London leads in institutional breadth (64 cultural assets, 1,023 suburban rail stations, 30 major sporting events hosted). Two fundamentally different strategies for building a world city, arriving at the same destination.
